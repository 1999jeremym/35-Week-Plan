35 Week Plan

Refeed

A refeed = one planned high-calorie day per week, eaten at or slightly above maintenance, with the extra calories coming primarily from carbs.

The specifics for you:

  • When: Your heaviest training day of the week. For most people that's leg day or the highest-volume upper day. Pick one day, keep it consistent.
  • Calories: ~3000 on refeed day (roughly maintenance for early phases, slightly below maintenance later as TDEE drops).
  • Protein: Same as always — 240g. Don't drop it.
  • The extra ~1200 cal vs your normal 1800 day: mostly carbs. Rice, pasta, oats, potatoes, fruit, bread. Not fats, not "treat foods."
  • Fat: Keep moderate. Don't make it a pizza day.

Why carbs specifically:

  • Refills muscle glycogen → better training performance the following days
  • Spikes leptin (the satiety/metabolism hormone that drops in a deficit) → blunts metabolic adaptation
  • Psychologically resets hunger and food obsession
  • Carbs are less calorie-dense than fat, so you can eat more food volume — which is part of the point

What a refeed is NOT:

  • ❌ A cheat day. "Refeed" ≠ "eat whatever you want." A 5000 cal binge undoes the entire week's deficit.
  • ❌ Junk food day. Pizza, ice cream, takeaway — these can fit in moderation if tracked, but the goal is structured carbs, not a free-for-all.
  • ❌ "Treat yourself" psychology. It's a metabolic and training tool, not a reward.
  • ❌ Optional when motivation is low. If you skip refeeds because you "had a good week," you'll stall by week 8.

Practical example refeed day for you:

  • Breakfast: oats (80g) + banana + protein shake = ~500 cal, 35g protein
  • Lunch: rice (200g cooked) + chicken breast (200g) + veg = ~600 cal, 50g protein
  • Pre-workout: rice cakes + jam + protein shake = ~400 cal, 30g protein
  • Post-workout: pasta (150g dry) + lean mince + sauce = ~800 cal, 50g protein
  • Dinner: potato (300g) + fish + veg = ~500 cal, 40g protein
  • Evening: Greek yogurt + Zilch ice cream + powdered PB = ~250 cal, 35g protein

≈3050 cal, 240g protein, mostly carbs. That's a refeed.

Frequency in your plan:

  • Weeks 1-2: No refeed. You're not deep enough yet.
  • Weeks 3-32: 1x per week, same day each week.
  • Week 10: The 5-day diet break replaces refeeds that week (you're at maintenance for 5 straight days).
  • Week 22: Same — 5-day break replaces refeeds.
  • Weeks 33-35: No refeed. Final push, deficit held tight.

One warning:

The first few refeeds, the scale will jump 1-2kg the next morning. That's water and glycogen, not fat. Don't panic. Don't skip the next refeed because "it set me back." It didn't. By 3-4 days later the water is gone and you're lower than before.

This is exactly the daily-weight-noise pattern I'm not going to engage with. Refeed Saturday → scale up Sunday → I don't want to hear it. Trust the weekly average.

Hydration & Electrolytes

If you're training first thing, you don't want to start sipping creatine during the workout — you want some of it in you before. Two reasons:

  1. Electrolytes pre-workout = better performance. Sodium specifically helps with blood volume and pump. Going in dehydrated tanks lifts.
  2. Creatine timing genuinely doesn't matter, but having it dissolved and ready means you actually drink it instead of forgetting.

Suggested flow:

  • Wake up: half a glass of plain water + metformin (if that's your morning dose schedule — confirm with your prescription).
  • Pre-gym (15-20 min before): 1/3 of the Owala. Gets electrolytes in, primes hydration.
  • During workout: sip another 1/3. Don't chug — sloshing stomach kills lifts.
  • Post-workout through afternoon: finish the rest.
  • Plain water on top: another 2-3L across the day.

One thing about morning fasted training in a deficit:

You'll be lifting on essentially zero food the night before plus a sleep fast. Some people thrive on this, some crash. Watch for:

  • Lightheadedness on heavy sets (especially leg day)
  • Strength noticeably lower than expected
  • Brain fog mid-workout

If any of these hit consistently in week 1-2: have something small 30-45 min pre-lift. A protein shake + banana, or Greek yogurt + honey. ~200-300 cal. You account for it in your daily 1800 — it's not extra calories, it's redistributed timing.

Don't preemptively change this. Try fasted first. Most people are fine. Adjust only if you see the symptoms above. The plan is the plan.

Progression rule: When you hit the top of the rep range across all working sets with good form, add weight next session. Example: chest press programmed 6-8 reps, you hit 8/8/8/8 on 60kg — next session, try 65kg for 6-7 reps and work back up.

PHASE 5-6 — WEEKS 18-32 (4x/week, same split, intensity techniques added)

Same template, but on the last set of select exercises, add one of:

  • Drop sets (e.g., chest press: hit failure at working weight, immediately drop ~30%, hit failure again). Use on 1-2 isolation exercises per session.
  • Rest-pause (hit failure, rest 15 sec, hit failure again, rest 15 sec, hit failure again). Use on machines, not free weights.
  • Myo-reps (similar to rest-pause but shorter rests, fewer reps). Advanced, optional.

Don't apply intensity techniques to compounds (leg press, chest press as a primary). Save them for isolations (curls, extensions, lateral raises).

Why now: as calories drop and recovery shrinks, total volume needs to come down slightly. Intensity techniques compress more stimulus into less volume — efficient.

PHASE 7 — WEEKS 33-35 (3-4x/week, dial back volume)

Final 3 weeks: protect what you've built, don't try to grow.

Option: Drop to upper-lower 3x/week (e.g., Upper Mon, Lower Wed, Upper Fri), or maintain 4x but reduce sets per exercise by 1.

  • Reduce intensity techniques. No more drop sets.
  • Stay in 8-12 rep range mostly. Less heavy work, more pump-style.
  • If a lift feels off, skip it. Recovery is shot, ego doesn't matter.

CARDIO STRUCTURE

You haven't actually been doing cardio in this plan — just steps. Steps are cardio, walking is the most underrated tool. But you green-lit adding zone-2 from week 18.

Steps (whole plan):

  • Wk 1-2: 8,000/day
  • Wk 3-6: 10,000/day
  • Wk 7-17: 10,000-12,000/day
  • Wk 18-24: 14,000/day
  • Wk 25-35: 15,000/day

How: walk to/from gym, walk during phone calls, walk after meals (also helps with metformin GI side effects), walking pad if you study sitting. Don't make it complicated.

Zone-2 cardio (weeks 18-35):

  • 2x per week, 30 minutes
  • "Conversational pace" — heart rate roughly 60-70% of max (~115-135 bpm for you)
  • Modes: incline treadmill walk (best — low impact, easy to maintain zone), stationary bike, elliptical
  • NOT running. At your size + lifting, running adds joint stress and recovery debt. Walking incline gets the same metabolic effect with less cost.

When to do cardio:

  • Best: separate session from lifting (e.g., morning lift, evening walk)
  • Acceptable: after lifting, never before
  • Worst: same time as legs day. Skip it leg day.

Why zone-2 specifically:

  • Burns calories without crushing recovery
  • Improves cardiovascular base (you're a nursing student — fitness on the floor matters)
  • Doesn't trigger same hunger spike as HIIT
  • Sustainable for 18 weeks

WHAT TO EXPECT FROM YOUR LIFTS OVER 35 WEEKS

  • Weeks 1-12: Strength up almost every session. Newbie gains. You'll add weight to the bar weekly. Enjoy it.
  • Weeks 13-20: Progress slows. PRs every 2-3 sessions instead of every session. Still gaining.
  • Weeks 21-28: Mostly maintaining. Some lifts still creep up, others stall. Normal.
  • Weeks 29-35: Strength may drop slightly. Goal is to lose <10% on any lift. If a major lift drops 20%+, that's a flag — recovery, sleep, or food is the issue.

Strength loss in late cut is not failure. Strength gain in late cut is not realistic. Maintenance is the win.

Recovery Activities

WHAT THEY ACTUALLY DO (AND DON'T)

Sauna:

  • ✅ Cardiovascular adaptation (real, modest)
  • ✅ Mental relaxation, sleep quality bump if used in evening
  • ✅ Some evidence for heat shock proteins, recovery
  • ✅ Feels good
  • ❌ Does NOT meaningfully burn fat. The "calories burned in sauna" figures are mostly water weight returning the second you drink fluid.
  • ❌ Does NOT detox. Your liver and kidneys do that. Sweat is mostly water + trace electrolytes.

Cold plunge / cold shower:

  • ✅ Mood / alertness boost (real, short-lived)
  • ✅ May help with DOMS perception
  • ✅ Mental discipline practice
  • ❌ Does NOT meaningfully boost metabolism. The "brown fat activation" calorie burn is real but tiny — ~50-100 cal max.
  • ❌ Cold immediately post-lifting blunts muscle protein synthesis and hypertrophy. This matters for you.

HOW TO INCORPORATE — RULES

Cold plunge / cold showers:

  1. Never within 4-6 hours of lifting. This is the big one. Cold exposure post-workout reduces muscle adaptation by ~15-20% in studies. You're cutting hard and trying to preserve muscle — don't sabotage the lifting. Save cold for non-training mornings or evenings.
  2. Best times: morning on rest days (Wed, Sat, Sun), or evenings on lift days after enough time has passed.
  3. Duration: 2-5 minutes is plenty. Longer doesn't help more.

Sauna:

  1. Post-lifting is fine. Heat exposure doesn't blunt hypertrophy the way cold does. Some evidence it may help via heat shock proteins.
  2. Hydrate aggressively. You're already on 1800 cal in a deficit. Sauna sweat = electrolyte loss. Use your Re-lyte bottle. Don't sauna fasted in the morning if you're already dehydrated overnight.
  3. Don't sauna immediately before sleep. Core temp needs to drop for sleep onset. Sauna 2-3 hours before bed = good. Sauna right before bed = disrupts sleep.
  4. Duration: 15-25 min is the sweet spot. Hotter ≠ better. Multiple short visits with cool-down between is better than one long burn.

ONE WARNING SPECIFIC TO YOUR CUT:

In late phases (week 25+), you'll be:

  • Lower body fat = less insulation
  • Lower calories = lower thermogenesis
  • Higher risk of cold intolerance as a warning sign

If you start finding cold plunges genuinely unbearable when they used to be fine, or if sauna leaves you dizzy/nauseous when it used to feel good — that's data. Could be electrolyte depletion, could be the early signs of metabolic adaptation we're watching for. Tell me when it changes, don't push through.

Also: don't sauna or plunge on diet break / refeed days if you don't want to. The body's already managing a lot of fluid shifts those days. Unnecessary stressor.
